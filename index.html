A living literary experiment

One poem.
One word at a time.
One hundred strangers.

Trace is not a social network, a writing platform, or a productivity tool. It is a place where language changes slowly, through hundreds of tiny human decisions.

A single poem lives here. It is already in progress. Strangers have already touched it. You may change exactly one word, any word, and then it belongs to the next person.

No accounts.
No profiles.
No likes.
No comments.
No followers.
No AI writing.

Just one human, one word, one quiet act of revision.

How it works

Read first. The poem is presented without editing tools. Reading comes before participation. Take your time with it.

Change one word. Tap any word in the poem. Type a replacement. Only substitution is allowed. No additions, no deletions. One word.

The poem seals at 100 revisions. When the final change is made, the poem becomes immutable. A naming ceremony begins. The community votes on a title drawn from the poem's own lines.

The archive grows. Sealed poems enter a permanent archive. Each one can be replayed from its very first word — a slow performance of every decision, in order, as it happened.

The philosophy

Trace asks whether authorship can be collective. It celebrates revision rather than creation. It values process over ownership.

Every participant leaves only the smallest trace. Yet together they create something larger than themselves — something no single person could have written or claim.

The archive is the artwork.
The process is the performance.
The participants are anonymous.
The poem belongs to everyone.
